Be bravewith Ukraine

The nature of Ukraine is very diverse. Here you can find amazing faunal complexes, represented by vast forests, endless steppes and meadows, majestic mountains and picturesque reservoirs.

Artifacts of the Tryplian culture testify to the fact that people lived on the territory of modern Ukraine as early as the fifth century BC.

Ukraine is the world's largest producer of sunflower seeds, and in every part of Ukraine you will see stunning fields of these yellow flowers - so many that they could cover the whole of Slovenia!

The world-famous Neapolitan song "O Sole Mio" was written by Eduardo Di Capua in 1898 in Odessa. He was in a southern port with his father, a violinist, when he was struck by a beautiful dawn on the Black Sea. Poet Giovanni Capurro wrote the words to this song soon after.

The Ukrainian Carpathians are rich not only in landscapes, delicious dishes and hospitality. Which Carpathians do you want to see? Flowered or snowy, green or yellow-hot? It all depends on what kind of vacation you are aiming for: hiking in the mountains under the scorching sun or the opportunity to engage in skiing or visit a spa.

Styles and monuments of folk architecture in different regions of Ukraine have separate histories and features. The architectural tradition of Ukraine has gone through a long and winding path of development, which continues even now: new modern buildings are being built in cities and old ones are being revitalized, giving them new life. Ukrainian architecture is a collection of everything that was built in Ukraine: from traditional mazankas to postmodern buildings in the 21st century.

Оur forests rank first in Europe in terms of biological species diversity. Spruce forests, where you can see rows of new generations stretching up from fallen tree trunks covered with moss. In the summer there are many blueberries and mushrooms.

And there is an endless number of picturesque places, architectural monuments and views that cannot be overlooked.
